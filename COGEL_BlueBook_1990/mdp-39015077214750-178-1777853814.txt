LOBBYING: COMPLIANCE AUTHORITY OF SELECTED AGENCIES
(As of January 1, 1990)
(m) Minnesota: Various.
(n) Minnesota: $1,000 per violation.
(0) Nebraska: Cease and desist.
(p) Nebraska: N.A.DC can initiate prosecution but attorney general and county attorneys have concurrent jurisdiction over criminal provisions.
(q) New Jersey and Oklahoma: Maximum $1,000 per violation.
(r) North Carolina: $10 late filing fee for expense reports.
(s) North Carolina: If convicted of a violation of provisions of lobby law, lobbyist shall not register for a period of two years following conviction.
(t) North Carolina: The NC Attorney General's Office investigates violations and the Wake County DA.S Office handles prosecutions.
(u) Oklahoma: Three times unreported expenditure.
(v) Oklahoma: District Attorney of Oklahoma City.
(w) Tennessee: Up to $10,000.
(x) Tennessee: Suspend lobbying license.
(y) Texas: $100 civil fine for untimely report.
(z) Texas: This office is not empowered to "request" prosecution, but it has some duty to inform appropriate prosecutors of certain problems. As for lobbyists, (1) We must notify
the Attorney General if the filer does not timely file a report and does not voluntarily pay the civil late fine. Sec. 254.042, Elec. Code. (2) Whenever we determine that a required
form was not filed, we must send written notice of that fact to the filer; if the filer fails to file the form within 21 days of such notice, we must file a sworn complaint with the
prosecuting attorney. Sec. 305.034, (Gov't Code).
(aa) Texas: Local state prosecutor (i.e., county of district attorney).
(bb) Vermont: With Attorney General.
(cc) Virginia: $50 per day.
(dd) Virginia: Richmond Commonwealth's Attorney and/or Virginia Attorney General.
(ee) Washington: $1,000 maximum for single violation; $2,500 for multiple.
(ff) West Virginia: Up to $1,000.
(gg) West Virginia: Reprimand, recommend termination of employment.
(hh) Wyoming: Misdemeanors.
(ii) District of Columbia: Up to $500 per violation.
(jj) District of Columbia: U.S. Attorney for the District of Columbia.
