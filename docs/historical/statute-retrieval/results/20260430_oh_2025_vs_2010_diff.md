## The `governors_office.required` flip

PRI item **A5 (Governor's office)** scored 0 in 2010, 1 in 2025 (per the r2 baseline run picked for projection).

Statute reading: **§101.70(F)** explicitly excludes the governor from the "legislative agent" definition:

> "Legislative agent" means any individual, **except** a member of the general assembly, a member of the staff of the general assembly, **the governor**, lieutenant governor, attorney general...

So the governor *as an individual* clearly does not register under Ch. 101. Whether **the governor's office** (as an institutional entity, e.g., when staff or a designee lobbies on behalf of the office) registers is a different question — and it depends on Ch. 121's "executive agency lobbyist" definitions, not Ch. 101's. The 2010 baseline read this as 0; the 2025 r2 run read it as 1. **Could be a real legal change in Ch. 121 since 2010, could be scorer judgment drift on the same statute text. Not investigated further in this MVP pass.**

## Scorer drift signals worth flagging for the extraction-harness branch

Even when 2 or 3 runs agree, the rubric/scorer combo has **systematic blind spots** that a real extraction harness must fix:

1. **Qualitative materiality is invisible to the current scorer.** §101.70(F)'s "main purposes" test is a real materiality gate but doesn't fit the rubric's quantitative-threshold shape. D0=0 in 2 of 3 runs (and D1_value/D2_value got hallucinated values) is the symptom.
2. **Conjunctive rubric items get scored disjunctively under prompt drift.** r1 read "address AND phone required" as 1 when only address is required. The 2010 prompt iteration that fixed this didn't fully transfer to the 2025 statute text.
3. **Section-level shrinkage in §101.70 / §121.60 is mostly a Justia hosting artifact** — the 2010 page contains *both* the pre-9/10/2010 and post-9/10/2010 versions of the section ("set out twice"), inflating the 2010 corpus by ~2000 words across two sections.

## What this artifact is and isn't

**Is:**
- A baseline OH SMR keyed to the 2025-vintage statute, comparable in shape to the 2010 baseline.
- The first multi-vintage-same-state SMR comparison the project has produced.
- Evidence that the OH disclosure law is structurally stable across 2010–2025 (modulo one likely-real and one likely-drift flip).

**Isn't:**
- A correct legal reading of OH disclosure law. The `de_minimis` nulls in both years are wrong (§101.70(F) "main purposes" qualitative test).
- A schema for a disclosure-extraction pipeline. PRI's rubric asks diagnostic yes/no questions; an extraction pipeline needs filing-field schemas (filing types × required fields × cadence × triggers × cross-references). That's the statute-extraction harness work.

## Substantive 2010→2025 statute changes detected at the text level (not via PRI projection)

From whole-text diffing the 30 core sections (chrome-stripped, quote-normalized):

- **§101.70 (Legislative Lobbying Definitions):** Ohio casino control commission and its officers/agents added to "person" and "legislative agent" definitions (likely 2012, post-casino legalization). Gendered language replaced ("his" → "the individual's"). Cross-citation updated (§2301.501-508 → §230.501-508 — likely a real renumbering elsewhere in the ORC).
- **8 procedural sections grew 15–60%** (§101.71/72/77/78/79/91/96/99 + §121.61/66/67/69 in parallel) — additions, not replacements. Almost certainly added enforcement / disclosure requirements over 15 years. **Not investigated section-by-section in this pass.**
- **12 sections ≥ 90% similar** — minor housekeeping only.
