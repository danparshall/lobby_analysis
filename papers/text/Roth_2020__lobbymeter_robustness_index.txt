Title: Creating a Valid and Accessible Robustness Index (Roth — lobbymeter.eu project)

Author: Aminata Sarah Roth

Summary
=======

Roth's research addresses shortcomings in existing lobbying-regulation
measurement tools by developing a new robustness index and an online
platform (lobbymeter.eu).

Problems Identified in Prior Indices
------------------------------------
Roth examined eleven existing robustness measures and identified critical flaws:

  - International applicability issues with some indices.
  - Poor balance between validity and reliability due to too many or
    too few items.
  - Equal weighting that failed to reflect the varying importance of
    different regulations.

New Index Structure
-------------------
The new framework contains 23 items organized into six sub-indices.
Roth applied differentiated weights across items to account for their
relative significance and to balance representation across dimensions.
Selection criteria included "face validity" and ensuring items
measured a single underlying concept rather than multiple unrelated
aspects.

Validation
----------
A convergent validity assessment showed a moderate correlation
(r = 0.6807) between the new index scores and Corruption Perceptions
Index data across 13 jurisdictions.

Lobbymeter.eu Platform
----------------------
The online resource offers:
  - Automated scoring calculations.
  - Country profiles for EU member states.
  - Educational content addressing misconceptions about lobbying.

A PDF containing the full index items is referenced on the page but
not embedded; downstream users must access the lobbymeter.eu platform
or contact the author for the full item list.
