## This past week

- **Dan** — Spent the week shifting from "compendium populated" to "compendium *projectable*." The 141-row compendium that shipped 2026-05-01 has been good enough as a contract for Track A and Track B, but it's still PRI-shaped under the hood (the atomization decisions inherited PRI's question hierarchy). The user-direction pivot 2026-05-02 was: **rebuild the compendium from each source paper independently** on a new branch (`compendium-source-extracts`), and judge the rebuild by whether each source rubric can be reconstructed via a per-rubric projection function from compendium cells. Six shipments this week:
  1. **Projection-success criterion landed and sharpened** — compendium 2.0 must satisfy four explicit commitments: ONE compendium (no rubric-specific variants), ONE extraction pipeline (uniform methodology across rows / states / years), multi-year reliable (OH 2010 / 2015 / 2024 / 2025, not just one), and **source rubrics as SANITY CHECKS on extraction accuracy, not the goal**. Per-rubric projections are the falsifiable test: populate compendium for state S in vintage Y → apply each rubric's projection → compare to that rubric's published per-state score. Match within tolerance ⇒ extraction is sound on the rows that rubric reads, in that vintage. **All rubrics must pass for the vintages they cover.**
  2. **3-way × 3-run consensus pass executed** (2026-05-07) — 9 parallel `general-purpose` subagents (M1 cluster-anchored / M2 blind / M3 FOCAL-anchored × 3 runs each) clustered 252 atomic items across rubrics; ~9 min wall-clock total. Strict consensus: 24 clusters / 63 items at ≥8/9 agreement. Loose: 39 clusters / 106 items at ≥6/9. 468 pairs in human-review band. M2 blind was the most stable method (14.4% within-method instability); M1 cluster-anchored noisiest (19.1%, surprising — the embedding prior was treated very differently across runs); M3 high (45.6%) but driven by big-group amplification.
  3. **CPI 2015 C11 atomic items recovered** — what was a single placeholder row in our archive turned out to have 14 atomic indicators in CPI's `criteria.xlsx` on the `PublicI/state-integrity-data` GitHub repo. 6 de jure + 8 de facto items extracted; bonus: per-state per-indicator scores for all 50 states × 14 indicators (700 ground-truth cells). Discovery: **8 de facto items use 5-tier scoring (0/25/50/75/100), not 3-tier as published criteria suggest** — CPI graders awarded 25/75 as scorer-judgment partial credit. The two-axis design in our v1.1 schema (legal_availability / practical_availability) accommodates this natively.
  4. **FOCAL flips from design-only to standard validation rubric** — Lacy-Nichols 2025 *Lobbying in the Shadows* applied FOCAL's 50 indicators to 28 countries' national lobbyist registers, **including US federal LDA** (81/182 = 45%). Wiley web-fetch was blocked on every route; manually downloaded Suppl File 1 + 2 (.docx); pandoc-converted; a second extraction agent populated all 50 indicators with verbatim weights, reconciled 1,372 per-country cells, and confirmed the US row sanity check. **Implication for project scope:** extraction jurisdiction expands from `{50 US states}` to `{50 US states} ∪ {Federal_US (LDA)}`. Federal LDA becomes the most-validated single extraction target — same jurisdiction as LobbyView.

     - **Sunlight 2015** (2026-05-11): 4 of 5 items in scope (item 4 excluded — 5-tier ordinal conflates 3-4 sub-features with a documented near-typo) → 13 distinct compendium rows touched. **11 of 13 rows have cross-rubric readers.** Sunlight's compendium contribution is validation redundancy on heavily-shared rows, not novel structure — exactly the projection-success criterion's "validated by many rubrics" pattern in action.
  6. **Three architectural conventions locked across all remaining Phase B mappings:**
     - **α — form-type split:** content cells split by registration form vs spending report. A state can require X on the spending report but not the registration form (HG Q5 vs Q20 is the canonical case); granularity bias pays the extra-row cost to preserve the distinction.
     - **β — projection-layer conjunction:** when a source paper bundles N conceptually-distinct observables into one item (Opheim's `disclosure.legislation_supported_or_opposed` conflates bill-identifier AND position), the conjunction lives in the projection logic, not by re-atomizing the source.
     - **"Collect once, map to many":** every compendium row is ONE statutory observable; multiple rubric projections read it. Every candidate row in remaining mappings carries a `[cross-rubric: <other readers>]` annotation. This is the seed for the compendium-2.0 dedup pass.

## Why this week mattered

**The compendium is moving from "populated" to "projectable."** Until this week, the compendium-as-shipped (141 rows) was a usable contract but its row design was structurally PRI-shaped — the 2026-05-02 v3 audit (186 concerns / 24.2% inter-auditor agreement) made that concrete and forced a rebuild from sources. The Phase B per-rubric mappings now in flight are the first concrete test of the rebuild: each rubric's items are walked against candidate compendium rows; the row design that emerges is what the rubric projection function can read.

**Three Phase B mappings in, the pattern is encouraging:** 11 of Sunlight's 13 rows are already read by other rubrics. The compendium-2.0 deduplication pass — which collapses redundant rows after all 9 mappings exist — has tangible evidence to work with. The projection-success criterion gives us a clear stop condition: minimum compendium that lets all 9 rubrics project correctly against their published per-state scores.

## Two reframings worth flagging

1. **FOCAL becomes a standard validation rubric, not design-only.** When FOCAL 2024 was just a checklist with no scoring application, it contributed only row design. Lacy-Nichols 2025 supplies cell-level scoring (yes/partly/no × weight) for 28 countries × 50 indicators, *including* US federal LDA at 81/182 = 45%. FOCAL now has the same shape as PRI, CPI, etc.: a projection function with published per-jurisdiction ground truth. Federal LDA enters the extraction-jurisdiction set as the most-validated single target.
2. **Source rubrics are SANITY CHECKS on extraction accuracy, not the goal.** This is the four-commitment sharpening of the criterion: the deliverable is a populated data layer (N canonical rows × {50 states ∪ Federal_US} × M vintages × {legal_availability, practical_availability} cells); per-rubric projections validate it. Multi-rubric × multi-year coverage gives redundant per-row ground truth (e.g., "expenditures benefitting officials" is read by Newmark2005 + Newmark2017 + Opheim + FOCAL.financials.10 — 4 independent checks on one row's extraction). Pluralism of rubrics ≠ ambiguity; it's the validation budget.
