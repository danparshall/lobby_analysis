## Entity Resolution & Record Linkage

- **Ornstein (2025)** — fuzzylink integrates pretrained text embeddings into an active learning probabilistic record linkage procedure, using LLM zero-shot labeling to dramatically outperform lexical-similarity-based methods on name/organization matching tasks. File: `papers/Ornstein_2025__fuzzylink.pdf`
- **Enamorado, Fifield & Imai (2019)** — fastLink: a fast, scalable R implementation of the Fellegi-Sunter probabilistic record linkage model that outperforms deterministic methods in FNR and estimation accuracy, scales to 160M+ records, and provides principled post-merge uncertainty quantification. File: `papers/Enamorado_2019__fastlink.pdf`
- **Libgober & Jerzak (2024)** — Introduces a half-billion LinkedIn employment record corpus to train ML and network-based organization name-matching methods, substantially outperforming fuzzy string matching on lobbying and financial datasets with up to 60% gains in F2 score. File: `papers/Libgober_2024__org_linking_open_collab.pdf`

## Lobbying Data Infrastructure

- **Bacik et al. (2025)** — Presents LobbyView, a relational database integrating 1.6M+ federal LDA reports ($87B+ expenditures) with entity disambiguation across clients, registrants, lobbyists, government entities, and legislators, linked to Compustat/Orbis/BoardEx/VoteView; this is the federal gold standard the state-level pipeline aims to replicate. File: `papers/Bacik_2025__lobbyview_network_dynamics.pdf`
- **Kim et al. (2025)** — Uses GPT-4 (with a graph neural network refinement layer) to classify interest group positions on federal bills from LDA report text, validated at 96.93% accuracy vs. human labels on 391 dually-annotated samples, producing 279k bill positions across 12k interest groups — 5× more groups and 7× more bills than MapLight. File: `papers/Kim_2025__ai_bill_positions_lobbying.pdf`
- **Kim (2018)** — LobbyView introduces a federal lobbying database linking interest group reports to congressional bills, with a technical description of the regex + cosine-similarity pipeline used to extract and disambiguate bill IDs from LDA report free text. File: `papers/Kim_2018__lobbyview.pdf`
- **LaPira & Thomas (2014)** *[stub-indexed]* — Constructs an original biographical dataset of ~1,600 federal lobbyists linked to ~50,000 quarterly LDA reports to measure revolving-door prevalence; documents that periodic LDA filings systematically under-disclose covered-official status. File: `papers/LaPira_Thomas_2014__revolving_door_lobbyists.pdf`
- **LaPira & Thomas (2020)** — Catalog of the structural quirks, artificial discontinuities, and systematic underuse of the federal LDA database across 25 years of filings, with guidance on correct levels of analysis, double-counting pitfalls, and the need for a lobbying data commons. File: `papers/LaPira_Thomas_2020__lobbying_disclosure_act_at_25.pdf`

## Compliance & Disclosure Quality

- **GAO (2025)** — 18th annual audit of federal lobbying disclosure compliance, finding 93–97% compliance on key filing metrics but a persistent 21% rate of improper covered-position disclosure and 63% of noncompliance referrals still pending as of December 2024. File: `papers/GAO_2025__lda_compliance_audit.pdf`
- **Lacy-Nichols et al. (2023)** *[stub-indexed]* — Scoping review (4,533 peer-reviewed and 285 grey-literature studies screened) cataloging the indicators, datasets, and methods used in academic lobbying research, intended to import political-science measurement into commercial-determinants-of-health work. File: `papers/Lacy-Nichols_2023__aiding_empirical_research_cdoh.pdf`
- **Chung, Cullerton & Lacy-Nichols (2024)** *[stub-indexed]* — 23-year OpenSecrets-data analysis of US federal lobbying spending by tobacco, alcohol, gambling, and ultraprocessed-food sectors (1998–2020), finding ultraprocessed food at $1.15B (top), tobacco $755M, alcohol $541M; concentration extreme (2 orgs = ~60% of tobacco spend). File: `papers/Chung_2024__mapping_lobbying_footprint.pdf`
- **Lacy-Nichols et al. (2024)** — Systematic scoping review of 15 lobbying transparency frameworks synthesized into FOCAL — an 8-category, 50-indicator rubric for evaluating what governments disclose about lobbying and how accessible those disclosures are. File: `papers/Lacy_Nichols_2024__focal_scoping_review.pdf`
- **Lacy-Nichols et al. (2025)** — Follow-up to FOCAL: applies the 50-indicator framework with 0/1/2 weighted scoring (182 points total) to all 28 countries with online lobbyist registers; Canada leads at 49%, US scores 45% (federal scope only, not state-by-state — flagged as future work); scope category scores highest across countries, revolving door + financials lowest. File: `papers/Lacy_Nichols_2025__lobbying_in_the_shadows.pdf`. **Supplementary:** `papers/Lacy-Nichols-Supple-File-1-IJHPM.pdf` → `papers/text/Lacy_Nichols_2025__lobbying_in_the_shadows__suppl_{001,002}.txt`

## State Lobbying Regulation Measurement (FOCAL source frameworks)

These works are the US-state-focused frameworks cited by Lacy-Nichols et al. 2024 as direct inputs to FOCAL (FOCAL refs #28–30, 33, 35). Together with PRI 2010 (also a FOCAL source) they represent the full set of state-focused scoring-rubric precedents feeding this project's unification work. CPI 2015 (State Integrity Investigation) is the source of the C11 atomic items extracted on this branch.

- **Opheim (1991)** — First systematic index of state lobby regulation: 22 binary items across three dimensions (statutory definitions 7, disclosure 8, oversight/enforcement 7), tested via OLS on 47 states; finds political culture (moralistic > individualistic > traditionalistic), staff support, and legislative pay predict regulatory stringency (R²=0.58). File: `papers/Opheim_1991__state_lobby_regulation.pdf`
- **Newmark (2005)** — Replicable 0–18 index of state legislative lobbying regulation across six time points 1990–2003 (17 binary items: statutory definitions, prohibited activities, disclosure requirements; +1–4 penalty supplement), validated at r=0.84 against Opheim 1991; mean state regulation rose from 6.54 (1990–91) to 10.34 (2003). File: `papers/Newmark_2005__state_lobbying_regulation_measure.pdf`
- **CPI "Hired Guns" (2007)** — 48-question, 100-point state ranking across 8 categories (Definition of Lobbyist 7, Individual Registration 19, Individual Spending Disclosure 29, Employer Spending 5, Electronic Filing 3, Public Access 20, Enforcement 15, Revolving Door 2); Washington topped at 87 (only state above 80). Web-only source; PDF printed from live page 2026-04-22. File: `papers/CPI_2007__hired_guns_methodology.pdf`
- **Clemens et al. / PRI (2010)** — Scores all 50 states on lobbying disclosure law quality (37 criteria) and data accessibility (22 criteria across 8 categories), producing state-by-state rankings; the accessibility rubric maps directly onto pipeline feasibility (machine-readable format, sortability, historical availability). File: `papers/PRI_2010__state_lobbying_disclosure.pdf`
- **CPI / Global Integrity — State Integrity Investigation (2015)** *[stub-indexed]* — Public-facing rollup article on the second-round 13-category state grades (245 questions); lobbying disclosure is one of 13 categories ("C11") — only Alaska, California, Connecticut earned above D+, 11 states earned F; this paper is the source for the C11 atomic lobbying-disclosure items extracted on the `compendium-source-extracts` branch. File: `papers/CPI_2015__sii_only_three_states.pdf`
- **Sunlight Foundation (2015)** — 5-category state lobbying disclosure scorecard with letter grades A–F (Lobbyist Activity, Expenditure Transparency, Expenditure Thresholds, Form Accessibility, Lobbyist Compensation); each category scaled −2 to +2. All 50 states scored with per-category points available as CSV. Web-only; Sunlight Foundation shut down 2020. File: `papers/Sunlight_2015__state_lobbying_disclosure_scorecard.pdf` (+ structured data in `Sunlight_2015__state_lobbying_disclosure_scorecard_data.csv`)
- **Newmark (2017)** — Updated 19-item 0–19 state lobbying regulation index for 2015 (primary-source statute review after Book of the States data collection ended in 2005); explicit comparison against Sunlight Foundation, CPI, and PRI finds the four disclosure measures largely measure different constructs (CPI ↔ PRI-disclosure r=0.04; new index ↔ PRI-accessibility r=0.27). File: `papers/Newmark_2017__lobbying_regulation_revisited.pdf`
- **OpenSecrets (2022)** — Direct successor to Sunlight's 2015 scorecard; 4-category, 5-point-per-category (20-point max) framework covering lobbyist/client disclosure quality, lobbyist compensation, timely disclosure, and public accessibility. Only 19 of 50 states have "meaningful" data in OpenSecrets' database (gated on compensation disclosure — 17 states require none, 7 partial, 26 full). File: `papers/OpenSecrets_2022__state_lobbying_disclosure_scorecard.pdf`

## International / Comparative Lobbying Regulation

Comparative and international frameworks, standards, and case studies — cited downstream by Lacy-Nichols 2024/2025 and Hogan/Murphy/Chari's *Regulating Lobbying* lineage. All entries below were added during the `compendium-source-extracts` branch and are stub-indexed.

- **Hogan, Murphy & Chari (2008)** *[stub-indexed]* — Interview-based study of unregulated-jurisdiction lobbying attitudes that builds the three-tier (low/medium/high) regulatory classification used by CPI Hired Guns and downstream comparative work; anchors the Opheim → CPI international lineage. File: `papers/Hogan_Murphy_Chari_2008__regulating_lobbying_globally.pdf`
- **Mihuț (2008)** *[stub-indexed]* — Comparative essay on the US (HLOGA 2007) vs. EU (European Transparency Initiative 2007 + Parliament Rules 1996-97) lobbying regimes, framed within pluralist vs. corporatist political-system theory. File: `papers/Mihut_2008__lobbying_us_eu.pdf`
- **Holman & Luneburg (2012)** *[stub-indexed]* — Comparative legal analysis documenting US/Canada disclosure-driven vs. early European access-driven lobbying registries, and tracking the European shift toward transparency post-scandal across Lithuania (2001), Poland (2005), Hungary (2006/2011), EC (2008), Macedonia (2008), France (2010), Slovenia (2010), Austria (2011). File: `papers/Holman_Luneburg_2012__lobbying_transparency.pdf`
- **ALTER-EU (2013)** *[stub-indexed]* — NGO coalition's reform recommendations for the EU Transparency Register: €10,000 financial-disclosure bandwidths, mandatory listing of prior public offices, drop-down legislative-dossier issue tags, fixed twice-yearly registration updates. File: `papers/ALTER_EU_2013__transparency_register_review.pdf`
- **AccessInfo / Open Knowledge / Sunlight / Transparency International (2015)** *[stub-indexed]* — Joint civil-society "International Standards for Lobbying Regulation" — 38 numbered principles across 7 areas (scope, transparency, integrity, participation, oversight, definitions, framework design); as of May 2015 only ~20 countries had national-level lobbying laws. File: `papers/AccessInfo_2015__intl_lobbying_standards.pdf`
- **SOMO (2016)** *[stub-indexed]* — NGO investigative report on Dutch Ministry-of-Finance lobbying capture by the financial sector, documenting the absence of binding Dutch lobbying disclosure and tracing influence channels through industry associations. File: `papers/SOMO_2016__structural_problem.pdf`
- **Transparency International EU (2016)** *[stub-indexed]* — Case study of the Integrity Watch (integritywatch.eu) platform, which combines ~7,000 EC senior-official lobby meetings with Transparency Register entries and MEP financial declarations via daily ParlTrack scraping; average lobbying-legislation quality across 19 EU countries assessed at 31%. File: `papers/TI_2016__open_data_eu_lobbying.pdf`
- **Council of Europe (2017)** *[stub-indexed]* — Committee of Ministers' Recommendation CM/Rec(2017)2 on legal regulation of lobbying activities — 10 guiding-principle sections (scope, freedom of expression, transparency, registers, ethics, sanctions, integrity, oversight, review) with explanatory memorandum, defining lobbying authoritatively for European member states. File: `papers/CouncilEurope_2017__lobbying_legal_regulation.pdf`
- **Bednářová (2020)** *[stub-indexed]* — Applies CPI Hired Guns (strength axis) jointly with Krsmanovic's CII (burden axis) to score the 2019 Czech draft Lobbying Act; pre-act regime CPI=0/CII=0, draft act would move Czech Republic to CPI=34/CII=35 ("medium" tier comparable to Slovakia/Poland/Hungary/Slovenia/Austria). File: `papers/Bednarova_2020__lobbying_regulation_quality.pdf`
- **Carnstone Partners / Meridian Institute (2020)** *[stub-indexed]* — Voluntary "Responsible Lobbying Framework" for companies and CSOs (not paid lobbyists), organized around five principles (legitimacy, transparency, consistency, opportunity, accountability) with detailed disclosure-content schema. File: `papers/Carnstone_2020__responsible_lobbying_framework.pdf`
- **McKay & Wozniak (2020)** *[stub-indexed]* — Empirical UK lobbying-transparency audit cross-matching the Office of the Registrar of Consultant Lobbyists' register against ministerial-meeting records (72,000+ meetings, ~1,000 clients/consultants over 4 quarters); only ~29% of register clients appear in meeting records, <4% of meeting-record groups in register. File: `papers/McKay_Wozniak_2020__lobbying_transparency_uk.pdf`
- **Roth / lobbymeter.eu (2020)** *[stub-indexed; web-source-only]* — Roth's 23-item, 6-subindex revision of the CPI Hired Guns / Opheim lobbying-regulation robustness index, validated against Corruption Perceptions Index at r=0.68 across 13 jurisdictions. **No local PDF — Google Sites page only**; WebFetch summary at `papers/text/Roth_2020__lobbymeter_robustness_index.txt`. Source: https://sites.google.com/view/regulating-lobbying/
- **Global Data Barometer (2022)** *[stub-indexed]* — First edition of a 109-country survey-based open-data index (May 2019 – May 2021 data) covering data governance, capabilities, availability, and use across multiple thematic modules including lobbying; no country scored over 70/100, mean 34.4. File: `papers/GlobalDataBarometer_2022__report.pdf`
- **IBAC (2022)** *[stub-indexed]* — Independent Broad-based Anti-corruption Commission of Victoria's special report on lobbying/donation corruption risks: identifies four lobbying-regime weaknesses (scope too narrow, privileged access, transparency gap, ineffective controls); Victoria is one of three Australian states with no campaign-spending cap. File: `papers/IBAC_2022__corruption_risks_donations_lobbying.pdf`

## Lobbying & Political Outcomes (empirical)

US state-level empirical studies that *use* lobbying-regulation strength as a treatment or moderator, rather than constructing the measure. Distinct from Category 4 (rubric-creating measurement papers).

- **Strickland (2014/2018)** *[stub-indexed]* — Panel-data analysis of all 50 states across three decades using an originally compiled lobbyist-registration dataset; finds campaign-finance restrictions (not reporting burdens) suppress registration, leaving "shadow" lobbyists unregistered and creating a reformer's paradox. File: `papers/Strickland_2014__lobbying_laws_interest_groups.pdf` (filename year is from working paper; actual publication date is 2018 per SAGE DOI 10.1177/1532673X18788049)
- **Flavin (2015)** *[stub-indexed]* — Uses National Annenberg Election Survey income-stratified opinion data with state policy outcomes to construct a "political equality" index across 50 states; finds that stricter state lobbying regulations are associated with a weaker income-influence gradient in state policy outputs. File: `papers/Flavin_2015__lobbying_regulations_political_equality.pdf`
