## Summary of LANDSCAPE.md

The core reframing: **this is a data-infrastructure project**, not a scoring system.

- **What we build.** Live, bulk-downloadable, cross-state lobbying disclosure data covering all 50 states on a weeks-to-months refresh cadence. OpenSecrets's 2022 state-lobbying scorecard acknowledges only 19 of 50 states provide "meaningful data" in their ingestion model — the 31-state gap is what we target. Companion output is an **N × 50 × 2 matrix**: for each field in a compendium × each state, is it *required* by law, and is it *available* (both legally and practically)?
- **Four-axis framing.** (1) Required, (2) Available, (3) Compliance, (4) Enforcement. We deliver axes 1–2 directly; axis 3 is enabled by our data (GAO does this federally — we make it possible for states); axis 4 is downstream of us entirely.
- **Why no "Corda Rubric."** Nine+ existing frameworks (PRI 2010, FOCAL 2024, CPI Hired Guns 2007, Newmark 2005/2017, Opheim 1991, Sunlight 2015, OpenSecrets 2022, LDA) measure overlapping-but-distinct constructs. Newmark 2017 found CPI and PRI disclosure scores correlate at **r=0.04** — essentially zero. So we union all of them into a field-level compendium and let downstream users apply their own weights. **We catalog, we don't grade.**
- **Where we fit in the civic-data stack.** Our models are Popolo-compatible at the reference-entity layer (Person, Organization, Identifier) and add lobbying-specific models (`LobbyingFiling`, `LobbyistRegistration`, `Gift`, …) that fill a real gap: the "Disclosures" OCDEP was withdrawn and no replacement was adopted. Natural distribution partner is **Open States** (operated by Plural); natural federal analog is **LobbyView** (Bacik et al. 2025, MIT, federal-only).
- **The sustainability bet.** CSG dropped the lobbying chapter from *Book of the States* around 2005; Sunlight Foundation's 2015 scorecard decayed when the org shut down in 2020. Both precedents say state-lobbying data collection was too expensive to maintain the old way. Our bet is that LLM-assisted extraction shifts the cost curve enough to make sustained maintenance viable.
